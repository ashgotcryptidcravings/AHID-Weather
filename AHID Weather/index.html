HOW WEATHER AFFECTS YOU
UV INDEX
UV radiation damages DNA in skin cells. UV 3+ causes sunburn in 30min for fair skin. UV 8+ can burn in 10min. Cumulative exposure increases melanoma risk. Eyes are vulnerable too — UV accelerates cataracts.
AIR QUALITY (PM2.5)
PM2.5 particles penetrate deep into lung tissue and enter the bloodstream. AQI 100+ triggers inflammation in airways. Long-term exposure above 50 correlates with cardiovascular disease. Asthmatics and those with respiratory conditions feel effects first.
HUMIDITY
Below 30% dries mucous membranes, increasing infection risk and worsening eczema. Above 60% impairs sweat evaporation — your body can't cool itself. Mold thrives above 70%, triggering allergies and respiratory issues.
HEAT INDEX (FEELS LIKE)
Combines temperature and humidity. Above 90°F: heat cramps possible. Above 105°F: heat exhaustion risk — dizziness, nausea, rapid pulse. Above 130°F: heat stroke territory — organ failure without intervention. Hydration is your main defense.
WIND CHILL
Wind strips body heat faster than still air. At 0°F with 15mph wind, exposed skin gets frostbite in 30 minutes. Hypothermia begins when core temp drops below 95°F. Layered clothing traps insulating air between layers.
BAROMETRIC PRESSURE
Rapid drops (>6 hPa in 3hrs) trigger migraines in susceptible people. Low pressure expands joint fluid, increasing arthritis pain. Some studies link pressure changes to mood shifts and fatigue.
DEW POINT
The real comfort metric. Below 55°F feels dry and comfortable. 55-65°F is noticeable. Above 65°F feels oppressive — sweat won't evaporate. Above 70°F is dangerous for prolonged exertion. More reliable than humidity percentage alone.
